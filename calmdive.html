Dive Response

in moments of extreme anxiety or distress it can be helpful to activate your dive response. a dive response is the body's natural reaction to being underwater, slowing down your heart rate to conserve oxygen and activating various diving reflexes.
by using this dive response distress and anxiety will decrease significantly, however not altogether.

to practice using your dive response it is best to use cool or cold water, and if possible to dunk your head in it for 1-5 seconds. washing your face thoroughly will do just fine.
regardless of how you practice it holding your breath will be essential to using this response. try to hold it for 5-10 seconds and take deep breaths after.
